Product Summary:

The Security and Privacy Requirements Analysis Tool (SPRAT) is a software tool designed to assist organizations in the analysis, specification, and management of security and privacy requirements for web-based systems. SPRAT provides a comprehensive platform for managing the entire requirements lifecycle, from initial goal and scenario definition to final requirements traceability and documentation.

Functional Requirements:

User Access Management: SPRAT will have an accessible user interface that allows users to log in and access the tool.
Goal and Scenario Management: SPRAT will provide a module for managing goals and scenarios, including the ability to define, track, and update goals and scenarios.
Policy Management: SPRAT will have a module for managing policies, including the ability to create, update, and track policies.
Flesch Readability Index Calculation: SPRAT will have a module for calculating the Flesch Readability Index of policies, providing an objective measure of policy readability.
Scenario Specification and Management: SPRAT will provide a module for specifying and managing scenarios, including the ability to create, update, and track scenarios.
Requirements Specification: SPRAT will have a module for specifying requirements, including the ability to create, update, and track requirements.
Legal Compliance: SPRAT will provide a module for managing legal compliance, including the ability to track and update legal requirements.
Requirements-level Access Control Analysis Framework (RACAF): SPRAT will have a module for conducting requirements-level access control analysis, including the ability to evaluate and enforce access control policies.
Non-Functional Requirements/Constraints:

Performance: SPRAT will be designed to handle a large number of users and process large amounts of data without significant performance degradation.
Security: SPRAT will be designed to ensure the security of sensitive data, including privacy policies, goals, scenarios, requirements, and access control policies.
Usability: SPRAT will be designed to be easy to use and intuitive, with a user-friendly interface that allows users to easily access and manipulate the data.
Scalability: SPRAT will be designed to scale horizontally and vertically as the number of users and data grows.
Compatibility: SPRAT will be designed to be compatible with various operating systems, browsers, and devices.
Accessibility: SPRAT will be designed to be accessible to users with disabilities and will comply with relevant accessibility standards.
Internationalization: SPRAT will be designed to support multiple languages and regions.
Error Handling: SPRAT will be designed to handle errors and exceptions gracefully and provide informative error messages to users.
Data Backup and Recovery: SPRAT will have a data backup and recovery mechanism to ensure that data is not lost in case of a system failure or data corruption.
Auditing: SPRAT will have an auditing mechanism to track and record user activities, including logins, logouts, and changes made to data.
Integration: SPRAT will be designed to integrate with other tools and systems, such as policy management systems, requirement management systems, and access control systems.
Customization: SPRAT will be customizable to meet the specific needs of different organizations and users.
Documentation: SPRAT will come with comprehensive documentation that includes user manuals, technical guides, and system administration guides.
Support: SPRAT will have a support mechanism, including user forums, FAQs, and technical support staff, to assist users with any issues or questions they may have.
Training: SPRAT will have a training mechanism, including online tutorials, webinars, and classroom training, to help users get started with the tool and learn its features and functionality.
